## What the data actually shows

For `mckp_N10000_M10_weakly_f0.500_seed0` the Lagrangian binary search
collapses onto λ ≈ 1.094–1.095 by N_iter ≈ 12 and is essentially
stationary thereafter:

| N_iter | λ_final | gap-to-B (cost − B) | optimality gap | HLD wall |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1  | 50.000 | −2 500 485 |  1.66 % | 73.14 s |
| 8  |  1.172 | −1 161 879 |  0.00 % | 88.44 s |
| 12 |  1.099 |    −62 748 | −0.00 % | 77.28 s |
| 16 |  1.094 |    +23 544 | −0.00 % | 110.76 s |
| 20 |  1.095 |       −897 |  0.00 % | 94.06 s |
| 25 |  1.095 |       −897 |  0.00 % | 89.24 s |

So the Phase-1 dual **does converge** on this instance — the per-record
"H1 fired" markers in `REPORT.md` reflect the unavoidable high
relative spread in the *first* 5 iterations of any bisection (the
window width is `min(5, N_iter)`), not a true degeneracy.

## Verdict on the two reviewer hypotheses

- **H1 (degenerate dual basis): NOT SUPPORTED on this instance.**
  Once N_iter ≥ 16, λ-spread drops below 1.1 % and stays there;
  by N_iter = 20 it is < 0.1 %. The "67 %" H1 flagging rate in the
  summary is dominated by early-iteration records (N_iter ∈ {2..16})
  where the bisection window is *expected* to be wide. Removing
  records with `n_iter < 8` drops the H1 rate to **20 %** (1/5
  records — the N_iter = 8 record only).

- **H2 (sub-MILP straggler): NOT SUPPORTED on this instance.** The
  slowest Phase-3 batch consumes 21–30 % of Phase-3 wall time — well
  below the 50 % threshold. The eight HiGHS sub-MILPs on N ≈ 1 250
  weakly-correlated classes are well-balanced.

## What the figure-4/figure-5 anomalies probably are

Two effects are visible even at seed 0:

1. **A small non-monotonicity at N_iter ∈ {2, 3}**: the gap *worsens*
   from 0.15 % → 0.18 % as N_iter goes from 2 to 3. The mechanism is
   not dual degeneracy — the dual is still mid-bisection at this
   point — but the Phase-2 estimated costs `C_k` are noisy reflections
   of a Lagrangian whose λ has only tightened by one bisection step.
   By N_iter = 5 the gap collapses (0.02 %) and the noise is gone.

2. **HiGHS reference suboptimality on hard instances**. HLD with
   N_iter ∈ {12, 16} found *strictly better* feasible solutions than
   our HiGHS reference (negative optimality gap), even though we asked
   HiGHS for `time_limit = 120 s`. Wall-clock confirms HiGHS actually
   ran for ≈ 705 s — the time-limit option is not honored during the
   pre-solve / heuristic phases on this instance. The reviewer-flagged
   anomaly in Figure 5 (HLD beating HiGHS at long horizons) is at
   least partly an artefact of how time-limited HiGHS reports its
   incumbent.

Both findings are useful for the manuscript: (1) gives a mechanistic
explanation for the small N_iter = 2-3 wobble that does **not** invoke
"degenerate dual basis", and (2) tightens the methodological framing
of Figure 5 (we should report HiGHS *status* alongside profit).

## Caveats / what's still gated

- **Single seed.** The full §4.3.1 spec calls for the deterministic
  anomaly subset over multiple seeds. Each HLD evaluation costs
  60–120 s on this cell, so the full sweep over 3 seeds × 25 N_iter
  values is ≈ 2.5 hours wall — deferred to a longer-running session.
- **Single instance class.** `(N=10 000, M=10, weakly, f=0.5)` only.
  The reviewer-flagged anomalies in Figs 4–5 are most pronounced in
  the inversely-strongly correlated cells; we should add at least one
  inversely-strong instance to the subset before §4.3.3.
- **HiGHS reference under-cooked.** Future runs should either (a) bump
  the reference time limit to ≥ 600 s and check `highs_status` in the
  metadata, or (b) cross-check with SCIP / Gurobi to make sure the
  "negative gap" records reflect HLD-beats-time-limited-HiGHS rather
  than a budget-rounding bug.
