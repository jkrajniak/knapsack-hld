The Phase-2 budget heterogeneity ``cv(B_k)`` is exactly linear in α
(0.000 → 0.015 across the grid), as the design specification
demands: at α = 0 every batch gets `B / K`; at α = 1 every batch
gets `B · C_k / C_total`; the mixed regime interpolates. The
empirical curve confirms the implementation matches the
specification, with no surprises.

## Verdict on the reviewer hypothesis

- **Fig 5 α ≈ 0.5 spike: NOT MECHANISTICALLY SUPPORTED.** With the
  tight-tolerance reference (`mip_rel_gap = 1e-9`), the mean gap at
  α = 0.5 is +0.0014 %, which is **lower** than the endpoint average
  +0.0027 % (α = 0 and α = 1). The standard deviation across seeds is
  also smaller at α = 0.5 (0.0005 %) than at either endpoint
  (0.0016 % at α = 0, 0.0023 % at α = 1). There is no Phase-2-induced
  sub-MILP regime change that produces a worse outcome at α ≈ 0.5;
  the mixed allocation is, if anything, the most robust point on the
  grid for this instance class.

The α = 0.5 wobble visible in the published Figure 5 is an
artefact of the HiGHS-default-tolerance reference used in the
original sweep (`mip_rel_gap ≈ 10⁻⁴`). HiGHS terminates with
`kOptimal` once the residual MIP gap is below ~10⁻⁴, leaving a
small pocket in objective space that HLD's per-batch MILP
composition can occasionally close. Whether HLD lands inside or
outside that pocket varies non-monotonically with α — purely
because the per-batch budget pressure shifts the sub-MILPs into
different parts of the search tree, not because α ≈ 0.5 is special.
Tightening the reference to `mip_rel_gap = 10⁻⁹` (§4.3.5) removes
that source of noise, and the gap-vs-α curve flattens into the
table above.

## What the wall-time data shows (and why one outlier is not the spike)

Mean HLD wall time is broadly flat in α (78–101 s across the grid),
with one outlier at **α = 0.5 on seed 0: 300 s**, where one Phase-3
sub-MILP consumed 59 % of the total Phase-3 wall time (vs ~25 %
typical). This is a single sub-MILP straggler on a single
(seed, α) pair — not a systematic α = 0.5 effect. The other two
seeds at α = 0.5 finished in 65 s and 85 s, and the wall-time
variance at α = 0.5 across seeds (σ ≈ 130 s) is dominated by this
one record. The straggler does **not** correspond to a worse gap
(seed 0, α = 0.5 gap = +0.0007 %, the median for that seed).

## Implications for the manuscript

§3.5 — proposed text (≤ 1 paragraph):

> The Phase-2 mixing coefficient α controls how much of the total
> budget B is split equally across batches versus proportionally to
> the Phase-1-derived per-batch cost estimate `C_k`. Empirically the
> per-batch budget coefficient of variation grows linearly in α
> (cv ≈ 0 at α = 0, cv ≈ 0.015 at α = 1), but the optimality gap is
> insensitive to this choice on the (N = 10 000, weakly correlated,
> f = 0.5) cell once a tight reference (`HiGHS.mip_rel_gap = 10⁻⁹`)
> is used: across α ∈ {0.0, 0.1, …, 1.0} and three seeds, the mean
> gap stays within [+0.0009 %, +0.0028 %] with no systematic
> dependence on α. The visible wobble around α ≈ 0.5 in the
> published Figure 5 is an artefact of the HiGHS-default
> `mip_rel_gap` tolerance (~10⁻⁴), which on these instances accepts
> incumbents up to 0.003 % short of the integer optimum. With a
> tight reference the curve flattens, and α = 0.5 becomes the
> *most* repeatable point across seeds (std 0.0005 % vs 0.0016 %–
> 0.0023 % at the endpoints). The default α = 0.9 retained in the
> remainder of the paper is therefore a stability choice — it is
> within statistical noise of the per-cell optimum on this archive,
> and the SMAC tuning campaign in §2.7 confirms that the optimal α
> is instance-class-dependent rather than universally 0.5.

## Caveats

- **Single instance class.** Only `(N = 10 000, M = 10, weakly,
  f = 0.5)` was swept. The α-anomaly is most pronounced in the
  inversely-strongly-correlated cells per Fig 5, but extending
  the sweep to one such cell (~3 h additional wall) is gated on
  the full-archive landing under §2.2.1.
- **Single instance per seed.** Three seeds is enough to refute a
  large Phase-2-induced spike, but the per-α standard deviations
  (0.0005–0.0023 %) are computed on n = 3 and should be read as
  rough indicators, not precise CIs.
- **Tight reference is timeout-bound on seed 0.** On this seed
  HiGHS at `mip_rel_gap = 1e-9` hit the 1 200 s wall limit
  without proving optimality. The negative-gap rows (e.g. seed 0,
  α = 0.7: −0.0009 %) reflect HLD finding a strictly better
  feasible solution than HiGHS-tight's accepted incumbent within
  the time budget. This makes seed 0 a positive evidence point
  for HLD on the hardest weakly-correlated cell, consistent with
  the seed-0 finding in the N_iter sweep.
